Objectives

This document presents a descriptive analysis of the time use of parents of children up to age 16 comparing men and women:

  • The time use activities should include working, parenting, caring, housework, sleep, social etc.
  • These could be broken down by highest qualification, social class, occupation, age banded, number of children etc.
  • In the first instance using 2014-15 UK TUS.

Analysis

There are a number of key notions to keep in mind when looking at time use data:

  1. The unit of observation is the day of a respondent. Each respondent may have one or two days worth of data - typically one weekday, one day at the weekend. Unless otherwise specified, results below are averaged across both days.

  2. There are two main approaches to estimating activity duration according to the denominator used:

  • Restricting the denominator to activity participants only. Estimates derived that way provide realistic values of duration for those who engage in the activity, but it has the disadvantage of relying on subsamples that may differ in composition, thus complicating direct comparisons.

  • Alternatively, one can use the full sample of relevant observations as the denominator, including respondents who reported no time in the activity. This approach enhances the robustness of comparisons between groups, but produces lower average duration estimates due to the inclusion of zero-minute observations.
